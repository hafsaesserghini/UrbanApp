Medical Emergency Solutions

Heart Attack

Quick steps to handle a suspected heart attack:

  • - Call emergency services immediately.
  • - Have the person sit down and stay calm.
  • - If possible, provide aspirin to chew.
  • - Perform CPR if the person is unresponsive until help arrives.

Severe Bleeding

What to do if someone is severely bleeding:

  • - Apply direct pressure using a clean cloth.
  • - Elevate the wounded area above heart level, if possible.
  • - Seek medical assistance immediately.

Stroke

Identifying and handling a stroke:

  • - Use the FAST method to identify symptoms (Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty).
  • - Call emergency services immediately.
  • - Keep the person calm and seated while waiting for help.
  • - Do not give food or drink in case of swallowing difficulties.

Choking

How to assist someone choking:

  • - Perform the Heimlich maneuver by standing behind them, wrapping your arms around their waist, and thrusting upward on their abdomen.
  • - If the person is unconscious, start CPR and call for help.
  • - For infants, use back blows followed by chest thrusts.

Burns

Steps to treat burns:

  • - Run the burn under cool water for 10-20 minutes.
  • - Do not apply ice or butter to the burn.
  • - Cover with a sterile, non-stick bandage.
  • - Seek medical help for severe burns.

Allergic Reactions

How to deal with allergic reactions:

  • - Administer an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) if available.
  • - Call emergency services immediately.
  • - Keep the person calm and seated.
  • - Monitor breathing and be prepared to perform CPR if necessary.
