Earthquake Emergency Solutions

During an Earthquake

Steps to stay safe during an earthquake:

  • - Drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy table, and hold on until shaking stops.
  • - Stay indoors until the shaking stops and it is safe to exit.
  • - If outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and utility wires.
  • - Avoid elevators and stairwells during the quake.

After an Earthquake

What to do after the shaking stops:

  • - Check yourself and others for injuries and provide first aid if necessary.
  • - Be prepared for aftershocks and move to a safe location if the building is damaged.
  • - Avoid using matches, lighters, or electrical switches in case of gas leaks.
  • - Contact emergency services if help is needed. Morocco Emergency Line: 15

Evacuation

When and how to evacuate safely:

  • - Leave the building if it is unsafe, using stairs instead of elevators.
  • - Carry an emergency kit with essentials like water, food, and a flashlight.
  • - Follow evacuation routes and listen to local authorities for guidance.
  • - Call for assistance if trapped. Morocco Civil Protection: 177

Emergency Kit Preparation

What to include in an emergency kit:

  • - Water (at least 1 gallon per person per day).
  • - Non-perishable food items and a manual can opener.
  • - Flashlights, batteries, a first aid kit, and essential medications.
  • - Copies of personal documents and a portable phone charger.

Communication During Emergencies

Stay connected and informed:

  • - Use text messages or social media to communicate instead of phone calls.
  • - Tune in to local radio or TV channels for updates.
  • - Inform family and friends of your safety through an emergency contact plan.
  • - For urgent assistance, call Morocco Emergency Line: 15 or Civil Protection: 177.
